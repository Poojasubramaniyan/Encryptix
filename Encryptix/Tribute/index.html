ABOUT Dr.A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied physics at the St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli.

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to the development of India's space and missile programs, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India".

He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.

ACHIEVEMENTS

  • Bharat Ratna (1997)
  • Padma Bhushan (1981)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997)
  • Veer Savarkar Award (1998)

INSPIRATION

Dr. Kalam's life and work serve as an inspiration to millions of people around the world.

His dedication to science, technology, and education has inspired generations of students and professionals.

His leadership and vision for India's development have made a lasting impact on the country.
